## What is a Fractional COO?
A Fractional Chief Operating Officer (COO) is an experienced executive who provides high-level operational leadership and strategy to your business on a part-time, retainer, or project basis. They bring the expertise of a seasoned executive without the burden of a $200,000+ full-time salary.

They are not just consultants who hand you a strategy deck and walk away. A fractional COO embeds themselves in your business, working directly with your team to execute the plan, build systems, and hold people accountable.

## 3 Signs You Need Operations Leadership
Many founders wait too long to hire operational help. Look for these warning signs:
1. **The Founder is in the Weeds:** You are spending 80% of your time on putting out daily fires — HR issues, supply chain hiccups, client complaints — and only 20% on strategy, sales, or product development.
2. **The Team is Scaling Past 10 People:** What works for a team of 4 completely breaks when you reach 12. Communication breaks down, decisions are delayed, and the culture starts to fray.
3. **Revenue Has Plateaued:** The business generates consistent revenue but margins are shrinking due to inefficiencies, or you literally cannot take on more clients without the system breaking.

## Fractional COO vs. Full-Time COO vs. Consultant
Here’s how to choose the right model:
*   **Consultant:** Best for identifying a specific problem and proposing a solution. They advise; they rarely execute.
*   **Full-Time COO:** Best for mature companies ($15M+ revenue) that need daily executive management and have the cash flow for a significant executive compensation package.
*   **Fractional COO:** Best for growing companies ($1M - $10M) that need strategic oversight to build systems, manage complex projects, and align the team, but don’t need 40 hours of executive time a week.

## What a Fractional COO Actually Does Week-to-Week
A fractional COO translates the CEO's vision into an executable plan. A typical week might involve:
*   **Strategic Planning:** Leading the leadership team in setting 90-day OKRs (Objectives and Key Results).
*   **Process Optimization:** Mapping out chaotic workflows (like employee onboarding or client intake) and building automated systems.
*   **Meeting Management:** Running effective, data-driven "Level 10" weekly meetings that actually solve issues rather than just sharing status updates.
*   **Team Development:** Mentoring mid-level managers and ensuring the right people are in the right seats.

## The Financial Reality: Typical Costs
In the Calgary market, a full-time experienced COO typically commands a base salary of $150K - $250K, plus bonuses, equity, and benefits. A fractional COO engagement usually runs between $3,000 and $8,000 per month, depending on the scope (e.g., 1 day a week vs. 3 days a week). This allows you to deploy capital toward marketing, product, or frontline staff while still securing executive talent.
